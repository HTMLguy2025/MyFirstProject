How to cook sausage


Ingredients:


  • Long sausage or horseshoe-shaped sausage
    (Don't use ground beef or ground sausage!)

  • A pan

  • A cutting board (If you have one, use a wooden
    cutting board instead of plastic)

  • Wooden or metal spatula
    (Wooden is a better option)

Instructions:


  1. On the cutting board, cut the sausage with a knife
    into circle-shaped pieces about a quarter inch thick.

  2. Cut the sausage pieces in half.

  3. Now heat up the pan until it feels
    slightly warm with your hand above it.

  4. Use the side of the knife to move the sausage
    off of the cutting board into the pan.

    • If the sausage isn't making any sound, turn up the heat.
    • If the sausage is sizzling, keep cooking it at that heat level.
    • If the sausage is smoking, turn down the heat and take the pan off of the hot part of the stove.
    • If the sausage is on fire, start panicking.

  5. Use the spatula to move the sausage around the pan. Try to cook every side of the pieces.

  6. When the sausage is darker than the original color
    but not black, it should be ready. If it looks like this, take the pan off of the hot section
    of the stove and serve. You can use the spatula to get the sausage out of the pan.
